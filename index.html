MLLM Planner · DiT Renderer · Unified Video Generation and Editing

Bernini Latent Semantic Planning for Video Diffusion

Bernini Team, ByteDance

Bernini is a unified framework for video generation and editing with self-supervised vision-text reasoning. It combines an MLLM-based semantic planner with a DiT-based renderer: the planner reasons over text, images, videos, and target tokens in a unified multimodal sequence, while the renderer synthesizes high-quality image and video outputs through diffusion-based flow matching.

V2V: Video Editing

Bernini performs instruction-guided video editing while preserving source structure, motion consistency, and low-level details. Source video features are used by the DiT-based renderer to maintain temporal consistency, while semantic edits are planned by the MLLM.

RV2V: Reference-guided Video Editing

Framework

Bernini contains two core components: an MLLM-based semantic planner and a DiT-based renderer. Multimodal inputs are serialized into a unified sequence of text tokens, source visual tokens, and target tokens. The planner performs iterative masked semantic refinement, and the renderer synthesizes final images or videos through flow-matching denoising.

MLLM-based Semantic Planner

The planner uses a multimodal large language model to reason over text, source images, source videos, and target placeholders. It progressively refines masked target tokens into complete semantic embeddings.

DiT-based Renderer

The renderer performs flow-matching denoising over VAE latent tokens. It is conditioned on semantic embeddings from the planner and source VAE features when available.

Segment-Aware 3D RoPE

SA-3D RoPE augments spatiotemporal rotary embeddings with segment-index-conditioned phases, allowing the model to distinguish tokens from different visual segments.
