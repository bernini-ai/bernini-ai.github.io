Bernini introduces a latent semantic planning paradigm for video diffusion. By organizing high-level motion, structure, and appearance intent in a compact planning space, Bernini enables controllable and temporally coherent video generation across V2V, RV2V, and S2V scenarios.

V2V: Video-to-Video Generation

Bernini transforms source videos while preserving temporal structure, motion identity, and scene continuity. The latent planner provides a semantic bridge between the input dynamics and the generated visual trajectory.

RV2V: Reference-guided Video-to-Video

RV2V uses reference visual evidence to guide appearance, identity, style, or scene attributes. Bernini aligns reference semantics with the source video trajectory through latent semantic plans.

Framework

Bernini separates high-level semantic planning from low-level video denoising. A latent planner first organizes motion, layout, subject, and scene semantics; the video diffusion backbone then realizes the plan into temporally consistent visual frames.

Latent Semantic Planning

Encodes high-level action, layout, appearance, and temporal intent in a compact planning space.

Task-unified Control

Supports video-to-video, reference-guided generation, and semantic-conditioned synthesis within one framework.

Temporal Coherence

Maintains stable identity, camera motion, object interaction, and scene continuity over time.
